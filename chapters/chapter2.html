Chapter 2 - The Sword's Edge

Tessa Greymourne watched the sun peak over the mountains from behind the aged wooden counter of the blacksmith as she lay her hammer on the sword she was forging. Sparks flew with every strike, small bursts of golden red light piercing the early morning darkness. The warmth of the fire flushed her face, which was a pleasant break from the stinging cold winds that whisked around outside.

She wrapped a damp cloth around her hand and grabbed the handle. The glow quickly vanished from the iron as Tessa dunked the sword into a basin of water. She wiped her brow and adjusted her leather apron. She was almost done with this quota for the Warmhaven guards.

“Tess! Do we have any newcomers at the counter?” her father called from the back room of the smith.

Tessa glanced up to find the last person she expected.

“Ah!” Dahrian?

“Hi, Tess,” she said quietly with a small wave. She was covered in snow for some reason.

“What are you doing out here so early?” she said, mildly alarmed. Tessa opened the gate and pulled her friend in by her coat. “Come in! You’re going to catch your death out there.”

“Who’s there?” her father questioned from behind the closed door.

“It’s just Dahri, Dad,” Tessa responded, brushing the snow off the top of Dahrian’s hood.

“Good morrow, Sir Greymourne,” Dahrian took off her cloak and set it on a nearby hanger.

“Bless you, child. No need for the flattery,” her father said with a deep chuckle, emerging from the back room.

“All is well, just got back from a walk,” Dahrian said politely, raising her eyebrows at Tessa on the word “walk.”

Tessa raised her eyebrows back as if to ask, What happened?

“Splendid, splendid,” her father said casually. “I need to go back to my work. Stay out of trouble girls.”

“Yes sir.”

There was a brief pause as Tess’s father went back into the office room.

“What happened?” Tessa finally asked.

“I’m getting married,” she said stiffly.

A deep well seemed to open in Tessa’s heart.

“Dahri, I’m so sorry,” was all she could manage.

“It’s the Earl’s son. The ceremony is in three weeks.”

“Earl Grenwood? I didn’t know he fathered another son.”

Dahrian giggled. “I actually just saw him on the road. He was pretty awkward.”

Tessa laughed. “At least you’ll live in a nice house now.”

Dahrian’s smile dropped. “But I don’t want to do that.”

The two girls exchanged glances of disgust.

“I understand your grievances,” Tessa said, stepping towards the hearth.

“He called me a wretch!” Dahrian threw up her hands.

“Agreed.”

Dahrian pulled up two chairs from the side of the room. They sat and watched the fire devour the logs.

“Tessa, have you ever thought of leaving this place?” her voice whispered.

“Like Snowfort?”

“No. Further. Omil maybe.”

“Dahrian… I just — I don’t like where you are going with this.”

“Just think about it, Tessa.”

“It’s not that simple.”

“I’ll… I’ll think about it,” she muttered.

“Great!” Dahrian clasped her hands.

“Dahri, I’m really not sure about this.”

“Why?”

Tessa stiffened. “That was different.”

“Stop. Please just stop.”

“It’s fine.”

The air was now icy and unwelcoming.

“Just go home, Dahrian.”

Tessa watched her friend leave.

Omil, eh?

She stared into the glassy mirror.

Her sixteenth year raced behind her eyes.

Your blood on your hands, Tess. That’s your blood.

She pressed her face into her hands.

She wasn’t there anymore.

And Mum was gone.

And she was twenty-three.

She unsheathed the small knife at her side and cut her hair into the basin.

Alden Black believed that blood was most useful when it stayed inside his body.

He didn’t mean to get stabbed.

This was all Perrin’s fault.

Well, Perrin, this is the last time.

Warmhaven.

They stayed for three days.

He slurred, “Load of oafs…”

Well, it’s been a good run.

I’m sorry father.

“Alden Black, you are under arrest.”

“Oh, great. Guards.”

Prison? Just let me die.

“At least I made it.”

“Bollocks.”
